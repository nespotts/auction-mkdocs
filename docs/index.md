## How to read this

- **FB Marketplace est.** = realistic private-party resale range (asking-to-selling) in the **NW Ohio / Toledo / SE Michigan / NE Indiana** market. This is what you could *flip it for*, not what to bid.
- **New cost** = current retail for a new (or closest current) equivalent — your reference for how much "runway" is in the item.
- **Est. hammer** = my estimate of the **gavel (winning bid) price** this lot likely brings at *this* sale — a well-advertised, 2-ring specialty sawmill + farm auction that draws targeted buyers. This is what it *sells for tomorrow*, before the 12% premium. It is typically **45–75% of the FB resale figure** (farm/tractor/container lots land high in that band; general shop tools and niche machines land low). **Add 12% for your real cost.**
- Toledo metro is a strong used-tool / farm-equipment market (industrial base, lots of woodworkers + farmers). Clean working stationary machinery resells at roughly **45–60% of new**; premium brands (Snap-On, Miller, Wood-Mizer) hold value better.
- **Single-phase 230V** items are worth more here than 3-phase — most farm/home-shop buyers don't have 3-phase power. That's a recurring value-add on this sale.
- Everything is **heavy, pickup-only iron** → realistic buyers are local. Containers/tractors/trailers have the widest buyer radius; benchtop tools the narrowest.
- **Condition is the dominant variable.** Every figure assumes the item runs and is complete (fences, knives, chisels, pumps, cables). Verify function at preview — completeness drives 20–40% of resale on shop tools.

### Region location context
Wauseon sits at the center of a dense ag + light-industrial buyer pool: Toledo (35 min), Fort Wayne IN (1 hr), Ann Arbor / Adrian MI (45–60 min), Defiance/Napoleon/Bryan OH (local). Forestry/sawmill and farm-implement demand here is healthy year-round; the niche pallet/stake machines (Lots 16–17) and the production sawmill (Lot 1) draw from a much wider regional/national buyer pool because so few come up for sale.
