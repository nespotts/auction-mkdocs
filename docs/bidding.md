# Bidding & methodology

## Bidding cheat-sheet (resale-minus-margin)

> **Your in-person edge:** no buyer's premium for you, but online bidders pay 12%. On contested lots you can bid ~11% past their gavel ceiling and still match their all-in cost — press that advantage on the big-ticket lots (1, 28, 36, 37, 38) where 12% is real money and online bidders feel it most.

To flip profitably, bid below the **low** end of the FB range (leave room for haul, cleanup, listing time, and a margin). Rough "don't pay more than" guidance for resale buyers:

- **High-value, broad-demand (bid 55–70% of FB low):** Lot 1 sawmill, Lots 36/38 tractors, Lot 37 chipper, Lot 28 forklift, Lot 41 trailer, Lots 39/40 containers. These are liquid enough to move; the risk is overpaying at a live auction caught up in bidding.
- **Premium tools that sell fast (bid 50–65% of FB low):** Lot 26 Snap-On box, Lot 18 Miller welder, Lot 25 Husqvarna saw, Lot 19 Jet helical planer, Lot 2/3 Wood-Mizer sharpening gear.
- **Niche / thin-market (be cautious — bid low):** Lots 16/17 stake machines, Lot 4 Baker groover, Lot 12 Whirlwind saw, Lot 14 Woodmaster — great upside *if* the right specialty buyer exists, but you may sit on them for months. Don't pay near FB value unless you have a buyer lined up.
- **Confirm-the-model lots before bidding:** Lot 9 (19" vs 24" bandsaw) and Lot 22 (single vs double-miter OMGA) — the value range doubles depending on the exact unit.

## Methodology & caveats

- **Provenance is a plus.** This is the dispersal of the former **Glen Garbers Sawmill** — many lots are genuinely "1 owner" with known service history (the LT70's recent engine overhaul, the low-hour tractors/forklift/chipper). A single-owner working-shop sale generally means better-maintained equipment than a mixed consignment auction.
- **How "Est. hammer" was derived:** scaled from the FB resale figure by item type, calibrated to a *well-advertised, 2-ring specialty sawmill + farm auction* that draws targeted buyers (sawyers, loggers, pallet shops, farmers). Broad-demand farm/tractor/container/trailer lots land high in the 45–75% band; general shop tools and thin-market niche machines land low. These are **gavel prices = your onsite cost** (no buyer's premium for floor bidders); **online bidders add 12%.** Live auctions are unpredictable — two determined bidders can blow past these, and orphan lots can fall well under.
- **Every estimate assumes the item is functional and complete.** Inspect at preview: run the sawmill/forklift/chipper/chainsaw, confirm pumps build pressure, check that fences/knives/chisels/cables/spindles are present. Non-functional collapses these values by 40–70%.
- **FB Marketplace and "buyer-to-remove" auction items have no public sold-price feed,** so estimates are triangulated from regional dealer asking prices, eBay/auction comps, and closest-class equipment. Dealer "reconditioned-with-warranty" prices (e.g., the $11,995 forklift, $2,800 pallet jack) are **ceilings** — private as-is sales run well below them.
- Several aggregators (MachineryTrader, ForestryTrader, eBay live listings) intermittently blocked automated fetches, so a few figures are search-surfaced summaries or *asking* prices rather than confirmed sold prices. True private-party sold prices typically land 10–20% below clean asking prices.
- **Lot-sheet corrections noted:** Lot 5 is an 80-gal compressor (not 8); Lot 9 and Lot 22 need model confirmation; Lot 12's single-phase spec is unusual for a Whirlwind 212 — verify the motor.
